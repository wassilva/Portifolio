Prague

Prague, capital city of the Czech Republic, is bisected by the Vltava River. Nicknamed “the City of a Hundred Spires,” it's known for its Old Town Square, the heart of its historic core, with colorful baroque buildings, Gothic churches and the medieval Astronomical Clock, which gives an animated hourly show. Completed in 1402, pedestrian Charles Bridge is lined with statues of Catholic saints.

How to get from Prague Airport to the City Center

The Prague International Airport Vaclav Havel is located about 10 kilometers northwest of the capital of the Czech Republic.

It is the main airport infrastructure of the country and one of the main hubs of Central Europe with about 11 million passengers transported last year.

To travel between the airport and the city center you can opt for several means of transport.

Airport Express

Airport Express is a bus that runs exclusively between Prague Airport and the city center with departures at Namesti Republiky metro station (yellow line) and at the Masarykovo Nádraží bus stop.

The Airport Express stop is located just outside Terminal 1. Follow the information on the airport information panels to find the stop.The frequency is every 30 minutes and the journey to the center takes between 30 and 40 minutes. The service runs from 5:45 am to 9:15 pm between the airport and the city center and between 06:35 and 22:05 in the reverse direction.

The ticket costs 60 CZK (about 2 Euro) and can be purchased directly from the driver.

There is also a cheaper option to use the metro and bus (numbers 100, 119 or 510) and cheaper (32 kroner). However, this alternative is impractical because it requires a change of means of transport and has a longer travel time.

Private Transfer

A more convenient, fast and safe option is to opt for the private transfer. This type of service can be booked in advance through the Czech Transport website, where it is necessary to fill out a form indicating arrival time, airline, passenger name, and destination hotel.


The fare is fixed and costs 600 kronor the 22 euros. Payment is made only at the end of the trip.

In the arrivals area there are also other companies offering the same type of service between 400 and 500 kroner. The cheapest option is offered by Prague Airport Shuttle from 290 kroner per person.

Taxi

Taxis are located just outside Terminals 1 and 2. There are two companies authorized to operate at the airport: Radiocab Taxi and AAA Radiotaxi.

A race between the city's airport can cost between 650 and 850 CZK (between € 25 and € 35) depending on the final destination..
